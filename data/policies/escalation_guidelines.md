## 1. Overview

This document provides guidelines for customer support agents on when and how to escalate issues beyond their standard resolution authority. Proper escalation ensures complex or sensitive issues are handled by the appropriate team.

## 2. Escalation Levels

### Level 1: Frontline Support Agent
**Authority:**
- Process standard returns and refunds per policy
- Apply store credits up to **$25**
- Extend return windows by up to **5 days** for documented extenuating circumstances
- Provide standard shipping cost waivers
- Reset customer passwords and update account information (with identity verification)

**Cannot:**
- Override system-blocked returns (final sale, past return window beyond 5-day extension)
- Issue refunds exceeding the original purchase price
- Apply retroactive promo codes or discounts
- Override fraud holds or account suspensions

### Level 2: Senior Support Agent / Team Lead
**Authority:**
- All Level 1 authorities plus:
- Apply courtesy credits up to **$75**
- Extend return windows by up to **15 days** beyond standard
- Override system-blocked returns with documented justification
- Apply one-time retroactive discounts (up to 15% or $50, whichever is less)
- Manage seller-customer disputes (first review)
- Authorize partial refunds on opened/used items at custom percentages

**Cannot:**
- Issue refunds over $500 without VP approval
- Make exceptions to final sale/no-return policy for restricted categories (hygiene, custom items)
- Override Trust & Safety decisions (fraud holds, account suspensions)

### Level 3: Support Manager
**Authority:**
- All Level 2 authorities plus:
- Approve refunds up to **$2,000**
- Grant exceptions to non-returnable categories on a case-by-case basis (with documentation)
- Override seller policies when buyer protection is warranted
- Authorize re-shipment of high-value orders without return receipt
- Approve escalated A-to-Z marketplace claims
- Issue compensation packages (product + credit) up to $200

### Level 4: VP / Director
**Authority:**
- All Level 3 authorities plus:
- Approve refunds of any amount
- Make policy exceptions at their discretion
- Handle legal threats, media complaints, and regulatory inquiries
- Authorize account reinstatement after Trust & Safety suspension
- Approve compensation exceeding $200

## 3. When to Escalate

### 3.1 Mandatory Escalation Triggers
The following situations **must** be escalated regardless of the agent's confidence:

| Situation | Escalate To |
|-----------|------------|
| Customer mentions legal action or attorney | Level 4 (VP) + Legal Team |
| Customer references media coverage or influencer status | Level 3 (Manager) |
| Order involves potential counterfeit product | Trust & Safety Team |
| Suspected data breach or unauthorized account access | Trust & Safety Team + Security |
| Customer reports discrimination or harassment by staff | Level 4 (VP) + HR |
| Order value exceeds $1,000 and issue is complex | Level 3 (Manager) |
| Customer is a verified business/enterprise account | Enterprise Support Team |
| Safety concern (product caused injury or property damage) | Level 4 (VP) + Product Safety Team |
| Government or regulatory inquiry | Level 4 (VP) + Legal Team |

### 3.2 Recommended Escalation Triggers
The following should generally be escalated, but agents may use judgment:
- Customer is highly upset after two resolution attempts
- Issue spans multiple departments (e.g., payment + shipping + seller)
- Policy is ambiguous for the specific scenario
- Resolution requires coordination with a third-party seller who is unresponsive
- Customer has extreme loyalty (Platinum tier, 5+ years, high lifetime value)

## 4. Escalation Process

### 4.1 Internal Escalation Steps
1. **Document:** Record the full issue history, actions taken, and reason for escalation in the ticket system
2. **Warm handoff:** When possible, introduce the customer to the next-level agent (don't just transfer)
3. **Set expectations:** Tell the customer the escalation timeline (e.g., "A senior agent will contact you within 4 hours")
4. **Follow up:** Check the ticket status within 24 hours to ensure the escalation was addressed

### 4.2 Escalation Response Times
| Escalation Level | Response Time |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Level 2 (Senior Agent) | Within **4 hours** (business hours) |
| Level 3 (Manager) | Within **8 hours** (business hours) |
| Level 4 (VP/Director) | Within **24 hours** |
| Legal Team | Within **24-48 hours** |
| Trust & Safety | Within **2 hours** (24/7) |

### 4.3 Customer Communication During Escalation
- Always confirm to the customer that their issue has been escalated and provide a reference/ticket number
- Provide an estimated resolution timeline
- Never promise a specific outcome ("I'll make sure you get a refund") — instead say "I've escalated this to a senior team member who has the authority to review your case"
- If the customer calls back before the escalation is resolved, any agent should be able to see the escalation status and provide an update

## 5. Escalation for Special Customer Segments

### 5.1 Platinum Loyalty Members
- All Platinum member issues should be handled by the **Platinum Concierge Team** (dedicated queue)
- If the Concierge Team is unavailable, escalate to Level 2 minimum
- Resolution authority for Platinum members is one level higher than standard (e.g., a Level 1 agent has Level 2 authority for Platinum issues)

### 5.2 Enterprise/Business Accounts
- Enterprise accounts have a dedicated Account Manager
- All issues should be routed to the Enterprise Support Team
- If the Account Manager is unavailable, escalate to Level 3

### 5.3 First-Time Customers
- Extra care should be taken with first-time customer issues
- If a standard resolution won't fully satisfy the customer, escalate to Level 2 with a note: "First-time customer — retention priority"
- Level 2 agents may offer a **$15 goodwill credit** in addition to the standard resolution

## 6. De-Escalation Techniques (Before Escalating)

Before escalating, agents should attempt these de-escalation techniques:
1. **Acknowledge the frustration:** "I understand this is frustrating, and I want to help resolve this."
2. **Summarize the issue:** Show the customer you understand by repeating the issue back.
3. **Offer the maximum resolution within your authority:** Don't escalate when you have the tools to resolve.
4. **Explain the 'why':** If a policy prevents the desired outcome, explain the reasoning clearly.
5. **Provide alternatives:** "While I can't do X, I can offer Y."
6. Only escalate when the customer's request genuinely exceeds your authority or the situation meets a mandatory trigger.
